About Standing Waves

A standing wave is formed when two waves of the same frequency and amplitude travel in opposite directions and interfere with each other. This simulation demonstrates two important cases:

Fixed End Reflection

When a wave reflects from a fixed end, it undergoes a phase shift of π (180°). This creates a node (point of zero amplitude) at the boundary. The incident and reflected waves cancel each other out at this point.

Free End Reflection

When a wave reflects from a free end, there is no phase shift. This creates an antinode (point of maximum amplitude) at the boundary. The incident and reflected waves reinforce each other at this point.

The resulting standing wave pattern shows nodes (points that never move) and antinodes (points of maximum oscillation) at fixed positions. The distance between adjacent nodes or adjacent antinodes is half a wavelength (λ/2).
